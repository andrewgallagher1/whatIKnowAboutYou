Digital Footprints:

Whenever you visit a website, you leave behind a digital footprint of information about you that that website can access without your permission. Digital footprints simply shows you just some of the things each website can see about you, depending on the browser that you use and what measures you have taken to minimise your footprint.

Minimising your footprint:

The majority of the information that a website can see about you relies of Javascriptâ€™s Navigator API. This API, although useful for some websites in providing full functionality so you can see what you want to see, can also give malicious ones this footprint about you, and also allow them to do some malicious things depending on the permissions you have set. By using a web browser extension that disables Javascript, you can minimise what a website can see about you.

As well as disabling Javascript, picking the right browser can determine how much information you give up. Depending on the browser you use, the results you get for each can be significantly different browser to browser. Having a look at the footprint each browser leaves behind, you can come to an easy decision on what browser you want to use.
